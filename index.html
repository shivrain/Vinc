The convergence problem

All four workstreams have to land on the same date — "locked picture" — when editorial is done changing the cut. After that, VFX has 6-18 months to finish. On a tentpole, that VFX window costs more than the entire shoot. This is the timeline AI most dramatically compresses, which is why every major incumbent and startup is pouring money in here.

Throughput math

Verdict: throughput math works comfortably. Constraint is NOT capacity. It's quality of bench and hiring velocity.
Three real constraints
Risk 1
Hiring velocity
8 → 60 in 12 months = 4–5 hires/month across 4 role types. Most pre-seed teams hit 20–25 in year 1, not 60. Risk: 12-month plan slips to 18. Series A timing slips accordingly.
Risk 2
Senior VFX supervisor bench
Om Prakash (RRR / Bahubali) is the only senior VFX sup on team today. Need 3–4 more for Pushpa-tier work. Risk: bottlenecks on QC sign-off. Senior VFX supervisor is the scarce, non-replaceable role — cannot be hired from LinkedIn.
Risk 3
Compute / GPU spend
18,888 hrs × inference + render = est. $1.5–3M/yr in CoreWeave-class infrastructure. NVIDIA L40S preferred. Risk: COGS pressure if GPU prices rise. Compute is 10–20% of revenue today and could compress as throughput scales.
Net verdict
Supply math supports demand comfortably at full build. But three real constraints create execution risk in the next 12–18 months. Series A milestone is hiring + bench, not contracts.

Six reasons the long-term trend favors the thesis
1
Strikes + Peak-TV correction, not demand destruction
2023 WGA/SAG strikes froze greenlighting into 2024. Streaming over-supply corrected. Disney/WBD deleveraged. One-time resets, not secular decline.
2
Forward curve is up and already turning
Ampere: global content $248B → $255B (2025-26). Streaming spend $95B → $101B. Streaming revenue heading past $200B by 2030. India: +1.6% in 2025 after the -9% year. FICCI-EY: Rs 105B → Rs 138B by 2028 (~10% CAGR).
3
VFX/post intensity per title is rising secularly
Episodic now carries film-grade VFX. Virtual production spreading. Shot counts per title climbing. Narrow VFX market forecast to double by 2034 (~10-11% CAGR). More of every budget flows to post even as budgets flatten.
4
AI is demand-expanding (Jevons paradox)
As cost-per-shot falls, more content uses VFX and more shots are attempted per title. Disney's Innovation lead + HDRI consultant both expect AI savings to be reinvested into quality/output, not banked as cuts. Volume up, not spend down.
(continued)
5
India gains share regardless of global pool size
30-50% cost advantage. Deepest talent pool. English workflows. Follow-the-sun delivery. AVGC mission. AI cost-curve rewards lowest-cost competent geography structurally. Even a flat global pool produces a rising India number as work migrates to lowest-cost hub.
6
A contracting incumbent pool is a tailwind for cost-disruptors
Studios under ROI pressure (worldwide box-office-to-cost 175% → 150%) are most willing to try a 10x-cheaper vendor in a downturn. Displaced senior artists are cheaper to hire. Capital-constrained incumbents move slowly. Downturns are when cost-disruptors take share.
Intellectual honesty on the steelman
Direction is up. Risks are timing and competition, not demand.
It requires three things to all break right:
  • (a) Recovery materializes roughly on schedule
  • (b) India converts AI cost advantage into share before the ~4,000 offshore vendors and the India-listed players (Basilic Fly, PhantomFX, Digikore) do
  • (c) Vinc diversifies beyond 4 studios so a single slate's timing can't whipsaw it
Net
Long-term trend favors the thesis. The 2024 contraction is the entry window, not the refutation.

The quality ceiling is Vinc's shelter AND its clock

Slide
15C
Five implications of the quality ceiling
1
It defines and bounds the wedge — to the SAM, not the TAM
AI usable for commercial/indie/OTT/regional but not hero work. Vinc's deliverable today is the Tier-2/3 repeatable tier + bottom ~80% of shots within theatrical films. Most of bottom-up volume but lower-value, most price-competed slice. Sizes Vinc to outsourced SAM, not full TAM.
2
Keeps Vinc a hybrid services business — for now
"80% there, last 20-50% manual" means human QA labor does not disappear. Margins stay labor-linked until the ceiling lifts. The software-platform upside ("Vinc Editor") cannot be underwritten on today's technology. It is an option, not a base case.
3
It is both moat AND time-bomb
While AI cannot do hero work, neither incumbents nor model labs can fully automate Vinc out — the ceiling shelters the services layer. But the day theatrical-grade gen AI arrives, the model layer (Runway / Adobe / Foundry) leapfrogs services entirely. Vinc is racing the removal of its own ceiling.
(continued)
4
It makes the data thesis the whole ballgame
If everyone is stuck at 80%, the only durable differentiator is whose proprietary data closes the last 20% fastest — which is exactly Vinc's accept/reject + edit-history corpus bet. The ceiling is the reason the data moat matters. Remove the ceiling and data stops being a moat.
5
The sharpest diligence tension
Vinc's anchors (Mythri, Sukumar) are theatrical — but theatrical is where AI is weakest.
Two readings:
(benign) Indian theatrical VFX is mid-complexity vs Hollywood hero work. Vinc takes the repeatable ~80% (roto/cleanup/comp) of those films, fully consistent with the ceiling.
(red flag) The claimed first-pass approval (20%→60%) and "10x-cheaper finished shots" are being applied to work the ceiling says cannot yet ship at theatrical quality.
Must verify by shot type on delivered work — not pitched work.
The binding question (Net of Parts 2 & 3)
The contracting-market worry is real but cyclical — direction is up. The quality ceiling is the binding constraint.
It caps Vinc to the mid tier. Keeps it a services business near-term. Converts the entire investment case into one question:
Does Vinc's proprietary data close the last 20% faster than anyone else's
— before the ceiling lifts and the model layer makes the services layer redundant?
